This is very likely teething-related, and here's why: during teething, babies produce significantly more saliva, swallow it constantly, and this can loosen and add mucus to stools. Given the 1–2 week timeline aligning with her new teeth coming in, this fits well. Since she has no fever, no blood, no vomiting, and is active — this is not a red flag, but do mention it at her next pediatrician visit if it doesn't improve in another week or so.
What can help firm things up:

Raw banana (Kachcha Kela) — this is your best friend right now. It's starchy, not sweet, binding, and high in resistant starch which is excellent for gut health AND weight gain. Cook it soft and mash it.
Moong dal khichdi — easy on the gut and binding.
Curd — the probiotics help regulate gut flora. Continue this daily.
Avoid excess high-fibre veggies in isolation (like too much spinach or cabbage at once) until stools firm up.


🥗 New Ingredients to Introduce
Things beyond what you're already using, all baby-safe and Indian-kitchen-friendly:
Carbs & Millets

Ragi (Finger Millet) — highest calcium of all millets, great for bone/teeth. Make as porridge with ghee.
Bajra (Pearl Millet) — iron-rich, healthy fats, good for weight gain. Soften well.
Jowar (Sorghum) — gentle on digestion, good fibre.
Kachcha Kela (Raw Banana) — as discussed above. A weight-gain and gut powerhouse.
Suji (Semolina) — easy to make into soft upma or porridge, calorie-dense.
Poha — soft, easy to eat, gentle on the tummy.
Potato — calorie-dense, mixes well into everything. Boil and mash into khichdi.

Pulses (beyond moong)

Masoor dal — cooks fastest, very digestible, high protein.
Chana dal — higher protein, introduce slowly.
Toor dal — classic, add ghee tadka for flavor and calories.
Sattu (roasted chana flour) — exceptional protein hit; make a savory porridge with ghee and jeera.

Veggies to add

Lauki (Bottle Gourd) — extremely easy to digest, great for loose stools, neutral taste she'll likely accept.
Tinda (Indian Round Gourd) — mild, soft when cooked.
Pumpkin (Kaddu) — works savory with jeera + ghee, not sweet when cooked this way. Good beta-carotene.
Beetroot — small amounts, mixed into khichdi. Iron-rich. (Don't be alarmed by pink poop — it's normal!)
Zucchini — very soft, neutral, easy finger food when steamed.
Ridge Gourd (Turai) — very gentle on digestion.

Fats & Calorie Boosters (critical for weight gain)

Ghee — already using. Increase slightly — 1 tsp per meal is ideal at this age.
Fresh coconut (grated/milk) — healthy fats, mild flavour, works in khichdi.
Til (Sesame seeds, ground) — calcium powerhouse, add as powder to porridges.
Flaxseed powder — omega-3, add a pinch to porridges.
Peanut butter (thin smear) — introduce carefully as a new food, calorie-dense.

⚖️ Weight Gain Priorities
Since she's active and appetite varies, these are the highest-leverage things:

Ghee at every meal — 1 tsp, non-negotiable. It's the single easiest calorie addition.
Kachcha Kela regularly — starchy, dense, gut-friendly.
Potato in lunch khichdis — invisible calorie booster.
Sattu 3–4x a week — protein density rivals meat.
Don't skip breastfeed/formula — at 11 months this is still a significant calorie source.
Offer food before a breastfeed when possible — she'll eat more solids when not already full from milk.
